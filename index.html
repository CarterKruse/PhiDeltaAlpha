About

Phi Delta Alpha was founded in 1884 as the New Hampshire chapter of Phi Delta Theta, a national fraternity. In January 1960, the Dartmouth chapter broke away from the national because the national would not allow minorities to pledge the house. The new, local fraternity replaced the last letter in its name with Alpha. In March 2000, the fraternity was derecognized by the college.

Under the leadership of Gig Faux, class of 1984, Phi Delta Alpha applied to the college for rerecognition in fall 2002. The first rush class was formed in the winter of 2003.

The fraternity is known for its strong commitment to philanthropy and community service, and its members are active in a variety of campus organizations and activities.
